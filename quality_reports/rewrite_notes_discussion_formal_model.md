## Diagnóstico da versão original

Three problems. First, the opening subsection (8.1) used a contrastive formula ("not that... it is that") and the vague phrase "extracts through information," omitting screening entirely. The author flagged this as substantively wrong. Second, two literature subsections (Steinberg, Maggi & Morelli) were separated by the unrelated Commitment subsection, breaking the comparative logic. Third, the Scope section's third paragraph speculated about endogenous erosion of informational power, which is material for a separate paper already identified in the project notes.
